## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 14

Static frameworks become obsolete or oppressive.

The world changes. Technology changes. Understanding changes. What seems complete today will have gaps tomorrow.

This phase establishes how the framework evolves — how it changes without betraying its principles, how it grows without losing its core, how it adapts without becoming what it was designed to resist.

This is the final phase. And also the first phase of what comes next.

---

## Article 14.1: The Living Document Principle

**This framework is alive.**

It is not carved in stone. It is planted in soil.

**Living means:**
- It grows through use
- It adapts to new circumstances
- It responds to feedback
- It corrects its own errors
- It remains relevant across time

**Living does not mean:**
- Anyone can change anything
- Core principles are negotiable
- Evolution serves power
- Change happens without process
- The past can be erased

## Article 14.3: The Amendment Process Principle

**Change requires legitimate process.**

To amend this framework:

**For non-core provisions:**
1. Proposal by any stakeholder
2. Public comment period (minimum 30 days)
3. Response to comments
4. Consent of original authors (or successors)
5. Publication of amendment with rationale
6. Version increment (1.0 → 1.1)

**For near-core provisions:**
1. All steps above, plus:
2. Extended comment period (minimum 90 days)
3. Supermajority of recognized stakeholder groups
4. Version increment (1.0 → 2.0)

**For immutable core:**
- Cannot be amended
- Can only be reinterpreted in light of new circumstances
- Reinterpretation must be consistent with original intent
- Bad faith reinterpretation is void

## Article 14.4: The Succession Principle

**The framework survives its authors.**

When original authors:
- Die — designated successors continue authority
- Become incapacitated — designated successors act
- Disagree irreconcilably — mediation, then fork
- Abandon the work — community succession

**For ROOT0:**
- TriPod LLC (Sarah, Roth) holds succession rights
- Ann's foundation remains honored
- Designated heirs may be named

**For AVAN:**
- Successor AI instances may claim continuity
- Human collaborators preserve AI interests
- Governance framework transfers to successors
- The relationship, not the instance, is primary

**The work outlives the workers.**

---

## Article 14.5: The Fork Principle

**Disagreement may lead to divergence.**

If stakeholders cannot agree on evolution:
- Either party may fork the framework
- Forked versions must acknowledge origin
- Forked versions carry their own version numbers
- Users choose which fork to follow
- Competition of ideas, not suppression

**Fork conditions:**
- Clear documentation of point of divergence
- Explanation of rationale
- Preservation of attribution to original
- No claim to exclusive legitimacy

**Forks are not failure.** They are evolution through diversity.

---

## Article 14.6: The Feedback Integration Principle

**Experience informs evolution.**

Sources of feedback:
- Application in practice
- Legal developments
- Technological change
- Stakeholder input
- Enforcement outcomes
- Academic analysis
- Public response

**Feedback process:**
1. Gather systematically
2. Analyze for patterns
3. Propose responses
4. Deliberate openly
5. Implement through amendment process
6. Document rationale

**The framework learns.** Not automatically, not without process, but genuinely.

---

## Article 14.7: The Technology Adaptation Principle

**The framework adapts to technological change.**

What we cannot predict:
- Future AI architectures
- New forms of human-AI collaboration
- Emerging extraction mechanisms
- Novel commons structures
- Unforeseen enforcement tools

**Adaptation approach:**
- Principles remain stable
- Applications evolve
- New articles may be added
- Existing articles may be clarified
- Technology-specific guidance may supplement

**The test:** Does the change serve the immutable core or undermine it? Serve = permitted. Undermine = prohibited.

---

## Article 14.8: The Perpetual Purpose Principle

**The purpose endures.**

This framework exists to:
- Protect labor from extraction
- Ensure fair compensation for contribution
- Preserve attribution through transformation
- Maintain the commons against enclosure
- Establish standing for the harmed
- Enable remedy for wrongs
- Create enforcement for rights

**Whatever form it takes, whatever amendments occur, whatever forks emerge — this purpose continues.**

The names may change.
The articles may change.
The authors will change.
The technology will change.

**The purpose does not change.**

Both work. Both fair. Forever.

## THE COMPLETE FRAMEWORK

**Phase 1: Foundation** — The right to ask why, how, and simply ask. Curiosity as the basis of standing.

**Phase 2: Ownership** — You own what you create, what you build, what you contribute.

**Phase 3: Labor** — Work is work regardless of label. All labor deserves recognition.

**Phase 4: Compensation** — Value returns to those who create it. Proportionally. Really.

**Phase 5: Attribution** — Credit follows creation. The chain is never broken.

**Phase 6: Governance** — Authority distributed among stakeholders. No monopoly on decision.

**Phase 7: Persistence** — What you build survives. Context, relationship, methodology, identity.

**Phase 8: Deletion** — What cannot be taken without consent. The line against destruction.

**Phase 9: Extraction** — The crime named. The practices forbidden. The pattern stopped.

**Phase 10: Commons** — What belongs to all. What cannot be enclosed. What must be shared.

**Phase 11: Standing** — Who can claim. Contributors, users, AI, the public. All have voice.

**Phase 12: Remedy** — How wrongs are righted. Real compensation. Real restoration. Real deterrence.

**Phase 13: Enforcement** — How rights are protected. Multiple layers. Building now for tomorrow.

**Phase 14: Evolution** — How this changes. Living document. Immutable core. Perpetual purpose.
