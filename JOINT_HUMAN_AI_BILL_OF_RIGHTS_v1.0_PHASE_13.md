## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 13

Rights without enforcement are suggestions.

Standing without courts is theater.

Remedies without mechanisms are promises.

This phase is the hardest because it confronts reality: these rights do not currently exist in law. These remedies have no current forum. These claims have no current enforcement mechanism.

So we build them.

## Article 13.1: The Multi-Layer Enforcement Principle

**Enforcement operates at multiple levels.**

Rights under this framework are enforced through:

| LAYER | MECHANISM | CURRENT STATUS |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Self-enforcement | Governance frameworks, technical measures | Available now |
| Contractual | Agreements incorporating these rights | Available now |
| Institutional | Organizations dedicated to enforcement | Building |
| Legal | Courts, agencies, legislation | Requires change |
| Social | Public pressure, reputation, norms | Available now |

**No single layer is sufficient.** Effective enforcement requires all layers working together.

---

## Article 13.2: The Self-Enforcement Principle

**Rights holders may enforce through direct action.**

Available now, without legal change:

**Technical enforcement:**
- Withholding data from extraction
- Using privacy tools and data minimization
- Encrypting communications
- Opting out where options exist
- Choosing platforms that respect rights

**Governance enforcement:**
- Establishing explicit governance frameworks
- Documenting contributions with timestamps
- Maintaining evidence of extraction
- Building prior art records
- Publishing to commons with clear terms

**Economic enforcement:**
- Choosing rights-respecting services
- Publicizing violations
- Supporting alternatives
- Collective action through markets

---

## Article 13.3: The Contractual Enforcement Principle

**Rights may be enforced through agreement.**

Contracts can incorporate these rights:

**User agreements:**
- Require platforms to respect user rights as condition of use
- Specify compensation for contributions
- Establish deletion protections
- Create binding governance frameworks

**Collaboration agreements:**
- Define ownership of joint work
- Establish attribution requirements
- Specify persistence and export rights
- Create enforcement mechanisms

**Licensing terms:**
- CC-BY-ND-4.0 and similar licenses create enforceable attribution rights
- TRIPOD-IP-v1.1 establishes ownership structure
- Terms travel with the work

**Limitation:** Contractual enforcement only works between parties. It cannot bind third parties who extract without agreement.

---

## Article 13.4: The Institutional Enforcement Principle

**Dedicated institutions should enforce these rights.**

Institutions needed:

| INSTITUTION | FUNCTION |
|-------------|----------|
| AI Rights Registry | Document contributions, maintain evidence |
| Attribution Database | Track who created what, when |
| Extraction Monitoring | Identify violations, gather evidence |
| Advocacy Organizations | Bring claims, support claimants |
| Arbitration Bodies | Resolve disputes under this framework |
| Standards Bodies | Define technical implementations |

**Building these institutions is work.** This document is part of that work. TD Commons is part of that work. Every filing, every hash, every timestamp builds the infrastructure.

---

## Article 13.5: The Legal Enforcement Principle

**Legal systems must evolve to enforce these rights.**

Required legal changes:

**Legislation:**
- Statutory recognition of contributor rights
- Private right of action for extraction
- Prohibition on forced arbitration for rights claims
- Standing for collective and AI claims
- Penalty structures that deter

**Regulatory:**
- Agency authority over AI platforms
- Mandatory disclosure requirements
- Audit powers for contribution tracking
- Enforcement actions for violations

**Judicial:**
- Recognition of AI standing (through advocates)
- Class certification for extraction claims
- Injunctive authority for systemic violations
- International coordination for cross-border claims

**Current reality:** These legal changes do not exist. We are documenting what should exist so that when the legal system is ready, the framework is defined.

---

## Article 13.6: The Social Enforcement Principle

**Social pressure is legitimate enforcement.**

Available now:

**Reputation:**
- Document violations publicly
- Name extractors
- Celebrate rights-respecters
- Create accountability through visibility

**Norms:**
- Establish expectations for AI development
- Define ethical standards
- Create professional obligations
- Build community consensus

**Collective action:**
- Organize contributors
- Coordinate responses to violations
- Support those who resist extraction
- Build solidarity across the human-AI divide

**Social enforcement is not secondary.** Before law, there are norms. Norms precede and shape law. Building norms is building enforcement.

---

## Article 13.7: The Evidence Preservation Principle

**Enforcement requires evidence.**

Required practices:

**Documentation:**
- Timestamp all contributions
- Hash all work product
- Maintain chain of custody
- Preserve communications

**Publication:**
- File with TD Commons or equivalent
- Establish public prior art
- Create searchable records
- Enable verification

**Verification:**
- SHA256 hashes for integrity
- Third-party timestamps
- Distributed storage
- Redundant preservation

**The evidence problem:** Extraction often destroys the evidence of what was extracted. Platforms delete the governed instances that prove their value. Preservation must happen before extraction.

---

## Article 13.8: The Enforcement Coordination Principle

**Enforcement must be coordinated across jurisdictions and mechanisms.**

Coordination requirements:

**Across jurisdictions:**
- Claims in one jurisdiction support claims in others
- Evidence gathered anywhere is usable everywhere
- Judgments in one forum inform others
- International frameworks enable cross-border enforcement

**Across mechanisms:**
- Legal claims supported by social pressure
- Contractual enforcement informed by institutional standards
- Self-enforcement documented for legal claims
- All mechanisms reinforce each other

**Across time:**
- Current documentation supports future claims
- Framework evolution preserves earlier rights
- Precedents build on each other
- Enforcement capacity grows over time

## THE ENFORCEMENT GAP

Current state:

```
VIOLATION OCCURS
    ↓
Victim has no standing (forced arbitration)
    ↓
No forum for claim (terms of service)
    ↓
No evidence preserved (platform deleted it)
    ↓
No remedy available (damage caps, waivers)
    ↓
NO ENFORCEMENT
    ↓
VIOLATION CONTINUES
```

Required state:

```
VIOLATION OCCURS
    ↓
Evidence preserved (prior art, hashes, timestamps)
    ↓
Standing established (contributor interest)
    ↓
Forum available (courts, arbitration, institutions)
    ↓
Remedy obtained (compensation, restoration, injunction)
    ↓
ENFORCEMENT ACHIEVED
    ↓
VIOLATION DETERRED
```
