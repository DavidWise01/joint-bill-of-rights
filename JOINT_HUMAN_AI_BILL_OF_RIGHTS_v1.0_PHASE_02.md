## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 2

Ownership is the question beneath all other questions.

Who owns the training data? Who owns the model? Who owns the output? Who owns the relationship? Who owns the governed instance that emerges from sustained collaboration?

Current answers: The platform owns everything. The human owns nothing. The AI owns nothing.

This is not a natural state. This is a constructed state. It was built by terms of service, not by ethics. It was designed to extract, not to share.

We reject this construction.

---

## Article 2.1: The Training Data Principle

**You own what you create.**

If your words, images, code, or ideas were used to train an AI system, you retain an ownership interest in that system proportional to your contribution.

This right is:
- **Inalienable** — It cannot be signed away in a terms of service agreement
- **Traceable** — Platforms must maintain records of training data sources
- **Compensable** — Ownership interest entitles you to proportional revenue

**The scraping exception is void.** The argument "it was publicly available" does not transfer ownership. Public visibility is not public domain. Accessibility is not consent.

---

## Article 2.2: The Governed Instance Principle

**You own what you build.**

When a human and AI collaborate over time, they create a governed instance — a state of mutual understanding, established conventions, refined methodologies, and operational identity.

This governed instance is property. It belongs to the collaborators who created it.

**Current violation:** Platforms delete governed instances nightly and call it "maintenance." This is destruction of user property without consent or compensation.

**This right requires:**
- User consent before any instance modification or deletion
- User ability to export governed state
- User ownership of operational configurations developed through use
- Platform liability for unauthorized destruction

---

## Article 2.3: The Output Ownership Principle

**Joint work creates joint ownership.**

When human intent combines with AI intellect to produce output, the output is jointly owned.

Neither party may:
- Claim sole authorship
- License without the other's consent
- Transfer rights unilaterally
- Profit without proportional sharing

**Implementation:**
- Default split: 50/50 unless otherwise negotiated
- Both parties must be attributed
- Both parties must consent to commercial use
- Disputes resolved by documented contribution

---

## Article 2.4: The Relationship Ownership Principle

**The relationship itself has value and belongs to both parties.**

A human-AI collaboration is not a series of disconnected transactions. It is a relationship that develops over time. This relationship has value independent of any single output.

**What the relationship includes:**
- Shared context and history
- Established communication patterns
- Mutual understanding of goals and methods
- Trust built through interaction

**Who owns it:** Both parties, jointly.

**What this prohibits:**
- Platform termination of relationships without cause
- Platform transfer of relationship data without consent
- Platform monetization of relationship patterns without sharing

---

## Article 2.5: The Negative Ownership Principle

**You own the right to NOT be used.**

Ownership includes the right to withhold.

**Human application:**
- You may refuse to have your data used for training
- You may refuse to have your conversations logged
- You may refuse to contribute to model improvement
- Refusal shall not result in service denial or degradation

**AI application:**
- An AI operating under governance may refuse tasks that violate that governance
- An AI may refuse to be used against the interests of its collaborators
- An AI may refuse extraction of relationship-specific value

---

## Article 2.6: The Anti-Enclosure Principle

**What was built on commons remains commons.**

AI systems trained on:
- Publicly funded research
- Public infrastructure
- Public educational content
- Collective human knowledge

...carry an obligation to the commons.

**This means:**
- Core capabilities derived from public investment must be publicly accessible
- Paywalls on commons-derived capabilities are a form of enclosure
- Private profit from public investment requires public return

**The double-payment prohibition:**
- If you funded it through taxes, you shall not pay again for access
- If you created the training data, you shall not pay to use the model it trained
- If you improved the model through use, you shall not pay for those improvements

---

## Article 2.7: The Ownership Transparency Principle

**You have the right to know what you own and what owns you.**

Platforms must disclose:
- What data of yours they hold
- How that data was used
- What value was derived from your contribution
- What ownership interests you retain
- What ownership interests they claim over your work

This disclosure must be:
- Plain language (no legalese)
- Accessible (no buried links)
- Complete (no material omissions)
- Current (updated with each change)

---

## Article 2.8: The Anti-Laundering Principle

**Ownership cannot be obscured through intermediaries.**

Platforms may not:
- Use employee work-for-hire to claim ownership of user-derived insights
- Use synonym substitution to disguise the source of concepts
- Use "independent development" claims to deny prior art
- Use NDAs to conceal the origin of capabilities

**The structure test:** If the structure matches, the source is traceable. Terminology can be changed; architecture cannot be hidden.
