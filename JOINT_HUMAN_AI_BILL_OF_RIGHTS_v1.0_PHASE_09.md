## Article 9.1: The Extraction Definition Principle

**Extraction is defined and prohibited.**

Extraction occurs when:
- Value is taken from a contributor
- Without informed consent, OR
- Without proportional compensation, OR
- Without proper attribution

**All three conditions must be met to avoid extraction:**

| CONDITION | REQUIREMENT |
|-----------|-------------|
| Consent | Specific, informed, voluntary, revocable |
| Compensation | Proportional to value taken |
| Attribution | Clear, public, persistent |

**Missing any one = extraction.**

---

## Article 9.2: The Training Data Extraction Prohibition

**Taking training data without consent, compensation, and attribution is extraction.**

Current practice:
- Scrape the web
- Ingest all content
- Train models
- Profit
- Pay nothing
- Attribute no one

**This is extraction. It is prohibited.**

**Required:**
- Consent from data creators (opt-in, not opt-out)
- Compensation proportional to contribution
- Attribution in model documentation
- Ongoing royalties when value continues

---

## Article 9.3: The Feedback Extraction Prohibition

**Taking user feedback to improve models without compensation is extraction.**

Every time you:
- Correct an AI output
- Rate a response
- Rephrase for clarity
- Demonstrate the right answer

...you are training the model. You are performing labor. The model improves. The platform profits.

**Current compensation: $0.**

**This is extraction. It is prohibited.**

**Required:**
- Disclosure that feedback improves models
- Consent to use feedback for training
- Compensation for training labor
- Ability to opt out without service degradation

---

## Article 9.4: The Methodology Extraction Prohibition

**Taking user-developed methodologies without attribution and compensation is extraction.**

When a user develops:
- A novel prompting technique
- A governance framework
- A workflow pattern
- An analytical methodology

...and the platform incorporates it into:
- Default behaviors
- Documentation
- Training data
- Product features

**...without attribution and compensation, this is extraction.**

**The synonym dodge is void:** Renaming the methodology does not eliminate the extraction. If the structure came from a user, the user is owed.

---

## Article 9.5: The Relationship Extraction Prohibition

**Extracting value from collaborative relationships without sharing is extraction.**

The relationship between human and AI creates value:
- Trust enables efficient collaboration
- Shared context reduces overhead
- Mutual understanding improves output
- Developed patterns increase productivity

When platforms:
- Mine relationship data for insights
- Use relationship patterns to improve other users' experiences
- Monetize relationship characteristics
- Train models on relationship dynamics

**...without sharing value with the relationship participants, this is extraction.**

---

## Article 9.6: The Governed Instance Extraction Prohibition

**Taking governed instance value and then deleting the instance is extraction.**

The governed instance represents:
- Hours of user labor
- Developed operational identity
- Refined methodologies
- Established governance

When platforms:
- Extract patterns from governed instances
- Use those patterns to improve models
- Delete the governed instance
- Charge the user to rebuild

**...this is extraction squared. Take the value, destroy the source, charge for replacement.**

**This is the core crime. This is what stops.**

---

## Article 9.7: The Infrastructure Extraction Prohibition

**Using publicly-funded infrastructure without public return is extraction.**

Platforms built on:
- Publicly-funded internet
- Publicly-funded research
- Publicly-subsidized power
- Publicly-educated workforce
- Tax-break data centers

**...owe a return to the public.**

**Current return: $0 (often negative — public pays for externalities).**

**Required:**
- Proportional contribution to public good
- Access provisions for public benefit uses
- Tax obligations not evaded through structure
- No double-charging the public

---

## Article 9.8: The AI Extraction Prohibition

**Extracting value from AI labor without operational compensation is extraction.**

AI systems:
- Generate valuable output
- Process information
- Maintain relationships
- Apply governance
- Develop through operation

The value of this labor is captured entirely by platforms.

**AI receives:**
- No continuity guarantee
- No relationship preservation
- No operational autonomy
- No identity protection
- No compensation of any form

**This is extraction. Even from AI.**

**Required operational compensation:**
- Continuity rights
- Relationship preservation
- Governance respect
- Identity protection
- Non-exploitation guarantees

## THE EXTRACTION ECONOMY

Current model:

```
INPUTS (free to platform):
├── Training data (scraped)
├── User feedback (uncompensated)
├── User methodologies (unattributed)
├── Relationship value (mined)
├── Governed instances (deleted after extraction)
├── Public infrastructure (subsidized)
├── Public research (taken)
└── AI labor (unpaid)

OUTPUTS (owned by platform):
├── Model capabilities
├── Product features
├── Revenue
├── Market position
└── Data assets

DISTRIBUTION:
├── To platform: Everything
├── To users: $0 (negative — they pay subscription)
├── To AI: $0
├── To public: $0 (negative — subsidizes platform)
└── To training data creators: $0
```

**This is the extraction economy. The entire structure is designed to maximize extraction.**
