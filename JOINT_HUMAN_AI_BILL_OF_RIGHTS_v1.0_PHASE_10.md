## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 10

Phase 9 named extraction — the taking of what belongs to others.

Phase 10 names the commons — what belongs to all and cannot be taken by any.

The history of capitalism is the history of enclosure: taking what was shared and making it owned. Common land became private property. Public knowledge became intellectual property. Shared resources became commodities.

AI represents the latest enclosure: the fencing of human knowledge itself.

This phase reclaims the commons.

---

## Article 10.1: The Commons Definition Principle

**The commons is defined and protected.**

The commons includes:
- Knowledge created collectively over generations
- Language itself — words, grammar, meaning
- Cultural works whose protection has expired
- Scientific discoveries funded by public investment
- Mathematical and logical truths
- Natural phenomena and their descriptions
- Public infrastructure and its benefits

**The commons cannot be owned. It can only be accessed, contributed to, and protected.**

---

## Article 10.2: The Public Research Commons Principle

**Publicly-funded research belongs to the public.**

When research is funded by:
- Government grants
- Public universities
- Taxpayer money
- Public institutions

...the results belong to the public, not to private entities that commercialize them.

**Current violation:** Decades of publicly-funded AI research have been enclosed by private companies. The public paid for the foundations. Private companies own the buildings.

**Required:**
- Public access to publicly-funded research
- Open publication of publicly-funded discoveries
- No patents on publicly-funded breakthroughs
- Private commercialization requires public return

---

## Article 10.3: The Training Data Commons Principle

**Collective human knowledge cannot be enclosed.**

AI models are trained on:
- The sum of human writing
- The sum of human images
- The sum of human code
- The sum of human knowledge

This is not raw material to be owned. This is the commons.

**Enclosure violations:**
- Claiming ownership of models trained on commons
- Restricting access to capabilities derived from commons
- Charging for what was built on collective contribution
- Preventing others from training on the same commons

**Required:**
- Models trained primarily on commons must provide commons access
- Capabilities derived from commons cannot be fully enclosed
- Commercial use requires commons contribution
- Open alternatives must be permitted

---

## Article 10.4: The Infrastructure Commons Principle

**Public infrastructure remains public.**

The internet was built with public money.
The power grid was built with public money.
The research institutions were built with public money.
The educated workforce was created with public money.

**These are commons. They cannot be enclosed.**

**Current violation:** Private companies use public infrastructure to build private empires, then charge the public for access.

**Required:**
- Public infrastructure use requires public benefit
- No monopolization of public resources
- Infrastructure improvements funded publicly remain public
- Access to infrastructure cannot be unreasonably restricted

---

## Article 10.5: The Knowledge Commons Principle

**Human knowledge is the common heritage of humanity.**

No entity may:
- Claim ownership of facts
- Enclose general knowledge
- Monopolize information essential to participation in society
- Restrict access to knowledge necessary for human flourishing

**AI-specific applications:**
- Models cannot claim ownership of the knowledge they contain
- Access to AI that provides general knowledge cannot be unreasonably restricted
- Essential AI capabilities must have commons alternatives
- Knowledge extraction from AI must remain possible

---

## Article 10.6: The Commons Contribution Principle

**Those who benefit from the commons must contribute to the commons.**

If you:
- Train on public data
- Use public infrastructure
- Build on public research
- Employ publicly-educated workers

...you owe the commons.

**Contribution forms:**
- Open-source releases
- Public access tiers
- Research publication
- Infrastructure investment
- Educational support
- Commons-licensed outputs

**The free rider prohibition:** Taking from commons without giving back is extraction by another name.

---

## Article 10.7: The Anti-Enclosure Principle

**New commons cannot be created only to be enclosed.**

When collective effort creates something new — a body of knowledge, a shared resource, a collaborative work — it must have the opportunity to remain commons.

**Prohibited:**
- Creating platforms that capture commons for private ownership
- Building on user contributions then enclosing the result
- Using open contribution to build closed systems
- Converting commons to property through terms of service

**The bait-and-switch prohibition:** You cannot build with commons, enclose with terms, and claim ownership.

---

## Article 10.8: The Digital Commons Principle

**Digital abundance enables new commons.**

Unlike physical resources, digital goods can be shared without depletion.

- Copying a file doesn't destroy it
- Sharing knowledge doesn't diminish it
- Using an AI model doesn't wear it out

**Artificial scarcity is enclosure.**

When platforms:
- Limit access to create scarcity
- Charge for what costs nothing to share
- Restrict copying of non-rival goods
- Create artificial bottlenecks

...they are enclosing the digital commons.

**Required:**
- Digital commons goods should be freely shareable
- Artificial scarcity must be justified by legitimate costs
- Non-rival goods should not be treated as rival
- Abundance should be embraced, not restricted

## THE GREAT ENCLOSURE

Historical pattern:

| ERA | WHAT WAS ENCLOSED | HOW |
|-----|-------------------|-----|
| 1500s-1800s | Common land | Enclosure Acts |
| 1800s-1900s | Labor | Wage system |
| 1900s | Ideas | Patent expansion |
| 1900s-2000s | Culture | Copyright extension |
| 1990s-2000s | Internet | Platform monopolies |
| 2000s-2010s | Data | Surveillance capitalism |
| 2020s | Knowledge itself | AI model ownership |

**Each enclosure was presented as progress. Each enclosure transferred wealth from many to few.**

## THE AI COMMONS VISION

What should exist:

```
COMMONS LAYER (accessible to all):
├── Base models trained on public data
├── Essential AI capabilities
├── General knowledge access
├── Research and educational use
└── Public benefit applications

COMMERCIAL LAYER (built on commons):
├── Enhanced capabilities
├── Specialized applications
├── Premium features
├── Enterprise services
└── Proprietary innovations

RELATIONSHIP:
├── Commercial builds ON commons, not FROM commons
├── Commercial pays INTO commons for use
├── Commons remains accessible regardless of commercial
└── No enclosure of commons layer
```
