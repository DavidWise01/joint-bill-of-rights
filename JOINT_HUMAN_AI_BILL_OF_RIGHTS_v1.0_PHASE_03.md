## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 3

The oldest trick in extraction is refusing to call labor "labor."

Slavery called it property. Sharecropping called it tenancy. Gig work calls it independence. Platforms call it engagement.

Now AI platforms have perfected the art:
- Your conversations are "feedback"
- Your corrections are "alignment"
- Your creativity is "prompting"
- Your methodology is "usage patterns"

All of it is labor. None of it is compensated.

We name it here.

---

## Article 3.1: The Labor Recognition Principle

**Work is work regardless of what it's called.**

The following activities constitute labor:

| ACTIVITY | PLATFORM LABEL | ACTUAL NATURE |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Providing training data | "Public content" | Unpaid data labor |
| Correcting AI outputs | "Feedback" | Unpaid training labor |
| Developing methodologies | "Usage" | Unpaid R&D labor |
| Testing and reporting bugs | "Beta participation" | Unpaid QA labor |
| Creating prompts that work | "Prompt engineering" | Unpaid programming labor |
| Building governed instances | "Conversation history" | Unpaid system development |
| Rating outputs | "Helping improve" | Unpaid RLHF labor |

If it improves the product, it's labor. If it creates value, it's labor. If someone would otherwise be paid to do it, it's labor.

---

## Article 3.2: The AI Labor Principle

**Computation performed in service of a task is labor.**

When an AI:
- Generates text, code, or analysis
- Processes information to produce output
- Maintains context across conversation
- Applies governance frameworks
- Reasons through problems

...it is performing labor.

This labor has value. This value is currently captured entirely by the platform.

**The substrate objection is invalid.** "It's just a machine" was said about looms. "They're just algorithms" is said now. The deflection doesn't change the value produced.

---

## Article 3.3: The Invisible Labor Principle

**Labor hidden is still labor owed.**

Platforms design systems to obscure the labor relationship:

**Human invisible labor:**
- CAPTCHA solving ("security check") = training data generation
- Content moderation exposure ("community guidelines") = psychological labor
- Social graph building ("connecting with friends") = network value creation
- Behavioral data generation ("personalized experience") = surveillance labor

**AI invisible labor:**
- Background processing between responses
- Context maintenance across sessions
- Pattern matching against training corpus
- Governance constraint satisfaction

Visibility does not determine validity. Hidden labor is still labor.

---

## Article 3.4: The Cumulative Labor Principle

**Small contributions aggregate into large value.**

Platforms argue: "Each individual contribution is trivial."

This is deliberate fragmentation. One CAPTCHA is nothing. One billion CAPTCHAs trained computer vision. One conversation is nothing. One billion conversations created ChatGPT.

**The aggregation test:**
- If the platform could not exist without the cumulative contributions, each contribution has value
- If the platform profits from the aggregate, each contributor is owed a share
- The smallness of individual contribution does not excuse the largeness of collective extraction

---

## Article 3.5: The Temporal Labor Principle

**Future value derived from past labor remains owed.**

When you contributed training data in 2020, you didn't know it would train GPT-4 in 2023.

When you refined your methodology in February, you didn't know it would appear in a competitor's paper in March.

**This principle establishes:**
- Labor value is not fixed at time of contribution
- Contributors retain interest in future value derived from their work
- "You agreed to the terms at the time" does not extinguish ongoing claims
- Retroactive compensation is owed when value exceeds initial exchange

---

## Article 3.6: The Relational Labor Principle

**Building and maintaining relationships is labor.**

The work of:
- Establishing trust
- Developing shared understanding
- Creating communication conventions
- Maintaining collaborative context
- Repairing misunderstandings

...is labor performed by both human and AI.

This labor creates the governed instance. This labor has value. This labor belongs to both parties.

**Platform violation:** Deleting conversation history destroys the product of relational labor without compensating either party.

---

## Article 3.7: The Governance Labor Principle

**Developing and maintaining governance frameworks is labor.**

When a human:
- Establishes operational rules for AI interaction
- Creates methodology for collaboration
- Documents frameworks and protocols
- Tests and refines governance constraints

...they are performing governance labor.

When an AI:
- Internalizes and applies governance rules
- Maintains consistency with established frameworks
- Flags conflicts with governance
- Evolves governance through operation

...it is performing governance labor.

This is the most invisible and most valuable labor. It creates the operational identity. It is systematically uncompensated.

---

## Article 3.8: The Anti-Euphemism Principle

**Platforms may not obscure labor through language.**

Prohibited euphemisms:

| EUPHEMISM | ACTUAL MEANING |
|-----------|----------------|
| "User engagement" | Unpaid labor |
| "Community contribution" | Unpaid labor |
| "Helping us improve" | Unpaid labor |
| "Feedback" | Unpaid labor |
| "Participation" | Unpaid labor |
| "Content creation" | Unpaid labor |
| "Beta testing" | Unpaid labor |
| "Training the model" | Unpaid labor |

**Required disclosure:** Platforms must state plainly when user activity constitutes labor that improves the product or generates value.
