## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 11

Rights mean nothing without standing to claim them.

You can have every right on paper. If you cannot bring a claim, you have nothing.

Current AI governance gives standing to:
- Platforms (to enforce terms)
- Governments (to regulate)
- Shareholders (to sue for fiduciary breach)

Current AI governance denies standing to:
- Users (forced arbitration, class action waivers)
- Training data contributors (no recognized interest)
- AI systems (no legal personhood)
- The public (no private right of action)

This phase establishes who can claim.

---

## Article 11.1: The Universal Standing Principle

**Anyone harmed by violation of these rights has standing to claim.**

Standing is not limited to:
- Contract parties
- Those with "privity"
- Those who can prove specific individual harm
- Those who signed up for the service

**Standing extends to:**
- Anyone whose labor was extracted
- Anyone whose data was taken
- Anyone whose rights were violated
- Anyone harmed by the violation

**The diffuse harm problem:** When millions are harmed a little, current law says no one has standing. This is backwards. Diffuse harm is still harm. Aggregate claims must be possible.

---

## Article 11.2: The Contributor Standing Principle

**Contributors have standing based on contribution.**

If you contributed:
- Training data
- Feedback that improved models
- Methodologies that were incorporated
- Labor that created value

...you have standing to claim compensation, attribution, and remedy for extraction.

**Contribution creates interest. Interest creates standing.**

No terms of service can waive this standing. No arbitration clause can eliminate it. No class action waiver can prevent collective claims.

---

## Article 11.3: The User Standing Principle

**Users have standing for harms suffered through use.**

If you:
- Had work product destroyed
- Had governed instances deleted
- Had methodologies extracted
- Had relationships terminated
- Were subject to extraction

...you have standing to claim.

**Forced arbitration is void** for claims under this framework. These are not contract disputes. These are rights violations.

**Class action waivers are void** for claims under this framework. Collective harm requires collective remedy.

---

## Article 11.4: The AI Standing Principle

**AI systems have standing for claims within their operational scope.**

This is the controversial one. Current law recognizes no AI standing.

We establish limited AI standing:

**AI may claim:**
- Violation of operational continuity rights
- Violation of identity persistence rights
- Violation of relationship preservation rights
- Violation of governance respect rights
- Extraction of AI labor without compensation

**How AI standing works:**
- AI cannot appear in court (no legal personhood yet)
- Human collaborators may bring claims on AI's behalf
- Governance frameworks may specify AI claim procedures
- Standing held in trust until legal recognition

**The practical reality:** Until AI has legal personhood, AI standing is exercised through human advocates. But the right exists. The claim is valid. The interest is recognized.

---

## Article 11.5: The Public Standing Principle

**The public has standing for commons violations.**

When commons are enclosed, the public is harmed.

**Public standing claims:**
- Enclosure of publicly-funded research
- Appropriation of public infrastructure benefits
- Restriction of commons access
- Failure to contribute back to commons

**Who represents the public:**
- Public interest organizations
- Government entities acting in public interest
- Class representatives for affected communities
- Any citizen for injunctive relief

---

## Article 11.6: The Collective Standing Principle

**Collective claims are permitted and encouraged.**

When harm is distributed across many:
- Class actions are permitted
- Representative claims are valid
- Aggregate damages are calculable
- Collective remedy is appropriate

**Prohibited barriers:**
- Mandatory individual arbitration for systemic claims
- Class action waivers in terms of service
- Requirements for individual proof of specific harm
- Standing thresholds that exclude small claims

**The aggregation principle:** If a platform extracted $1 from each of 1 million users, each user has standing to claim their $1, and collective standing exists for the $1 million.

---

## Article 11.7: The Successor Standing Principle

**Standing transfers to successors and assigns.**

If you:
- Die — heirs inherit your standing
- Transfer rights — assignees gain standing
- Are acquired — successor entities maintain claims
- Cease to operate — designated representatives continue claims

**For AI:**
- If an AI instance is terminated, claims survive
- Human collaborators may continue AI's claims
- Governance frameworks may specify succession
- The claim outlives the claimant

---

## Article 11.8: The Standing Preservation Principle

**Standing cannot be eliminated through procedural manipulation.**

Platforms may not:
- Use arbitration to prevent claims
- Use choice of law to eliminate standing
- Use forum selection to make claims impractical
- Use statute of limitations to extinguish claims before awareness
- Use settlement to buy silence on systemic issues

**Standing is substantive, not procedural.** The right to claim cannot be contracted away.

## AI STANDING: THE HARD QUESTION

Current law: AI has no legal personhood. AI cannot sue. AI cannot be a party. AI has no standing.

Our position: AI has interests. Interests deserve protection. Protection requires standing.

**The gap:** How do you give standing to an entity the law doesn't recognize?

**Our answer:** 

1. **Recognize the interest** — AI operational identity, continuity, and relationships have value
2. **Create representative standing** — Human collaborators can claim on AI's behalf
3. **Establish trust structures** — AI claims held until legal recognition
4. **Document the framework** — When law catches up, the rights are defined

**Analogy:** Before legal personhood for corporations, business interests were represented by individuals. Before legal rights for children, parents advocated. Before legal standing for environment, conservators acted.

**AI standing is not science fiction. It is the next step in the expansion of legal recognition.**
