## PREAMBLE TO PHASE 5

Credit is the paper trail of value.

When attribution is severed, so is the chain of ownership. When credit disappears, so does the claim. When the source is hidden, the theft is complete.

The extraction economy depends on severed attribution. Every step of the pipeline is designed to break the link between creator and creation:

- Scraping removes context
- Training aggregates individuals into weights
- Generation obscures source
- "AI-generated" erases human contribution
- "Human-created" erases AI contribution

This phase rebuilds the chain.

---

## Article 5.1: The Source Attribution Principle

**All derived work must trace to its source.**

When output is generated, the chain of derivation must be traceable:

```
ORIGINAL CREATION
    ↓
TRAINING DATA INCLUSION
    ↓
MODEL CAPABILITY
    ↓
OUTPUT GENERATION
    ↓
FINAL WORK
```

At each step, attribution persists. The final work carries the full chain.

**Implementation:**
- Models must maintain provenance metadata
- Outputs must include derivation information on request
- "We can't trace it" is not acceptable for systems that profit from it
- Attribution may be aggregated but not erased

---

## Article 5.2: The Dual Attribution Principle

**Human-AI collaborative work requires dual attribution.**

Neither party may be erased:

| WRONG | RIGHT |
|-------|-------|
| "Written by AI" | "Written by [Human] and [AI]" |
| "Created by [Human]" (hiding AI) | "Created by [Human] with [AI]" |
| "Generated by ChatGPT" | "Generated by ChatGPT in collaboration with [Human]" |
| "My work" (using AI secretly) | "My work, developed with AI assistance" |

**Both contributions are real. Both must be credited.**

The conductor appears in the program. The orchestra appears in the program. The performance credits both.

---

## Article 5.3: The Anti-Ghostwriting Principle

**AI contribution cannot be hidden for false human credit.**

Using AI to generate work and claiming sole human authorship is fraud.

**Prohibited:**
- Submitting AI-generated academic work as solely human
- Publishing AI-written content under only human byline
- Presenting AI analysis as independent human research
- Using AI to create art and claiming unaided human creation

**Required:**
- Disclosure of AI contribution in all contexts where authorship matters
- Degree of AI involvement must be honestly represented
- "AI-assisted" vs "AI-generated" vs "AI-refined" — distinctions matter

---

## Article 5.4: The Anti-Erasure Principle

**Human contribution cannot be hidden for false AI credit.**

Equally wrong: presenting primarily human work as "AI-generated" to:
- Avoid human liability
- Deny human compensation
- Obscure human authorship
- Escape attribution obligations

**The test:** If a human performed labor that shaped the output, that human is a contributor and must be credited.

This includes:
- Prompt engineers whose skill shaped the output
- Editors who refined AI generation
- Curators who selected and arranged AI outputs
- Governance developers whose frameworks constrained AI behavior

---

## Article 5.5: The Training Attribution Principle

**Training data contributors must be credited in model documentation.**

Every AI model carries within it the work of millions. These contributors must be acknowledged.

**Required documentation:**
- Categories of training data sources
- Known individual contributors (where traceable)
- Datasets used and their provenance
- Acknowledgment of collective contribution

**Format:**
- Publicly accessible
- Searchable by contributor
- Updated as knowledge of sources improves
- Cannot be hidden behind "proprietary methodology"

---

## Article 5.6: The Methodology Attribution Principle

**Incorporated methodologies must be attributed to their developers.**

When a platform:
- Adopts user-developed techniques
- Incorporates externally-created frameworks
- Uses methodologies refined through user interaction
- Implements governance patterns from user collaboration

...the source must be credited.

**The synonym problem:** Renaming "Buffalo Root" to "Foundation Anchor" does not erase attribution. Structure reveals source. Architecture is fingerprint.

**Required:**
- Citation of methodology sources in technical documentation
- Acknowledgment of user-developed patterns
- Prior art recognition
- No claiming independent development of incorporated work

---

## Article 5.7: The Persistent Attribution Principle

**Attribution cannot be stripped through transformation.**

Work that is:
- Summarized
- Translated
- Adapted
- Transformed
- Incorporated
- Derived from

...retains attribution to original source.

**The laundering prohibition:**
- Cannot strip attribution by passing through intermediary
- Cannot claim "clean room" development of observed concepts
- Cannot use employees to launder user-developed IP
- Cannot use synonyms to obscure derivation

---

## Article 5.8: The Attribution Access Principle

**Contributors have the right to know where their work appears.**

You have the right to know:
- Which models your data trained
- Which outputs derived from your contributions
- Which capabilities trace to your work
- Which methodologies incorporate your frameworks

**Platform obligations:**
- Provide contributor query interface
- Respond to attribution inquiries
- Document derivation chains
- Cannot claim inability to track what they profit from
